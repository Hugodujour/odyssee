Côtes de Provence

Rosé

Grape varieties

  • 45% Grenache

  • 25% Cinsault

  • 20% Carignan

  • 10% Mourvèdre

Terroir

The vines grow in clay-limestone soils formed during the Triassic geological period.

Vinification & Aging

Direct pressing for Cinsault and Mourvèdre. Cold maceration for Grenache.

Tasting

A lovely pale pink color, almost crystalline. The nose offers floral notes of lime trees, thyme, and broom, along with fresh fruit aromas of raspberry, strawberry, and blackberry. The palate is indulgent, elegant, and refreshing. A delicate wine that is easy to enjoy.

Data sheet

Bordeaux

Red

Grape variety

  • 100% Merlot

Terroir

The vineyards thrive on clay-siliceous soils resulting from the erosion of slopes on the high plains, and clay-limestone soils on the hilltops.

Vinification & Aging

After destemming, the grapes are placed in temperature-controlled vats to undergo fermentation, which lasts for 15 to 18 days. After racking, the wines are blended and aged in underground tanks.

Tasting

It boasts an intense and deep purple hue. On the nose, it unveils captivating aromas of stone fruits and cherry, subtly enhanced by spicy undertones. In the mouth, it unfolds with generous opulence and exquisite roundness, its tannins perfectly enveloped. Its finish stretches gracefully, distinguished by a revitalizing freshness.

COMMITMENT III

Data sheet

Sauvignon Blanc

White

Grape varieties

  • 70% Sauvignon Blanc

  • 30% Sauvignon Gris

Terroir

Clay-siliceous soils resulting from the erosion of hillsides on high plains, and clay-limestone soils on hilltops.

Vinification & Aging

Controlled at low temperatures throughout fermentation, the wine is then aged on fine lees for 3 to 6 months.

Tasting

A beautiful pale yellow color with green reflections. The nose is aromatic, initially with citrus notes and then exotic notes. The palate is fresh, delicate with a lovely silky texture and a longpersistence.
